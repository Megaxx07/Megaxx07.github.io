Research on Credit Risk of Listed Companies Based on GRBF and KMV Model
#Research 2020-12-01

Advisor: Professor Gu Guangtong (ZAFU)
12/2020 – 06/2021

Motivation and Objectives
Initially, my motivation for exploring this combined model was to effectively evaluate a company’s risk of default and to filter through numerous stocks for portfolio construction. This stemmed from my active involvement in the Chinese stock market and a deep interest in automated processes. Prolonged monitoring of the market can lead to inaccurate judgments, therefore, I aspired to introduce a neural network model to provide rational analysis and guidance for assessing a company’s risk of default.

Project Initiation
Completed this project independently. Furthermore, the integration of neural networks and risk assessment models with the KMV model in this project is an area that few have ventured into from 2020 to 2021.

Model Composition
Utilized a combination model consisting of the Generalized Radial Basis Function Neural Network (GRBFNN) and the credit risk evaluation model KMV. Its primary objective was to assess the credit default risk of 50 publicly listed companies in the Chinese stock market and recommend modeling enhancement.

Data Collection and Preparation
In the context of the Chinese stock market, randomly selected 50 publicly listed companies from each rating level. Obtained their 2020 annual financial report as well as stock price data from April 2020 to March 2021. Subsequently, data cleaning was conducted, and the dataset was divided into a training set and a test set with a ratio of 1:4.

Future Selection
Ten essential financial indicators were selected from 36 basic financial metrics using a factor analysis model, and their factor scores were computed. After analyzing scree plots and variance explained by the factors, the number of common factors explanatory variables was determined to be two.

Factor loading matrices were then calculated, and principal component analysis (PCA) was applied to identify two primary factors: one related to solvency and the other to profitability.

Model Fine-Tuning
In the modeling phase, fine-tuned the GRBFNN model, adjusting the number of hidden layers, input layers, and output layers. The test set data was input to the model for debugging, and model performance was assessed using loss functions and difference surface plots to detect signs of overfitting or under-fitting. Subsequently, the training set data was fed into the GRBFNN model, with input layers including common factors and factor scores, and output layers encompassing asset value and volatility.

Performance Evaluation
Finally, the KMV model to compute default probabilities and default distances for all publicly listed companies in the training set, which were then compared with market-released credit ratings. The average accuracy of the test set reached 74.46%. It is noteworthy that companies with higher credit ratings exhibited relatively lower accuracy.

Conclusion
In conclusion, this independently executed combined model can reasonably assess the risk of default for publicly listed companies. Given that this model was developed when neural network models were not widely popular, it still holds considerable potential for further advancement.
