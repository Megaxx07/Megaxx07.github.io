Research the Dynamic Correlation between Gold Futures and Spot Price under COVID-19
#Project 2020-09-22

Advisor: Assistant Professor Liu Baoseng (ZAFU)
09/2020 – 02/2021

Introduction and Objectives

This project as part of a financial engineering practical training course, was a collaborative effort involving myself and two team members. The primary objective was to empirically examine the correlation between gold futures and spot prices during the COVID-19 pandemic. Aimed to compare the performance of these assets during this unique period with unaffected gold futures and spot markets. The analysis sought to determine whether gold still maintained its value and risk-avoidance properties during the pandemic and explored the feasibility of cross-market arbitrage between gold futures and spot markets. Additionally, the project involved empirical analysis of volatility interdependence between gold futures and spot markets using the DCC-GARCH model in R. It also included portfolio trend forecasting based on 1% and 2.5% VaR by using GARCH-Copula CVaR approach.

Data Selection and Preprocessing

For the sample study, we selected daily trading price data for the main gold futures contract on the Shanghai Futures Exchange and the closing price of spot gold (Au9999) on the Shanghai Gold Exchange. The sample period covered January 1, 2019, to May 26, 2020. We conducted data cleaning and then performed descriptive statistics.

Statistical Testing and Causality Analysis

We initiated the analysis by conducting Augmented Dickey-Fuller (ADF) tests and cointegration tests to assess data stationarity and Granger causality tests to explore the causal relationship between gold futures and spot gold returns. The results revealed that spot prices Granger-cause futures prices, indicating that spot gold prices have a certain guiding effect on futures prices.

Impact Analysis and Variance Decomposition

In the next phase, we processed the returns of both gold futures and spot prices, introducing a shock to analyze their different responses during various time periods. We then conducted a variance decomposition for the returns of both gold futures and spot prices to assess the contribution of each variable to the changes in the two markets. The following visual representation illustrates our findings:

· The left chart reveals that spot gold has a consistent and stable influence on the volatility of the gold futures market. The contribution of spot gold to market volatility tends to stabilize at approximately 50%.
· The right chart demonstrates that the spot gold market has been increasingly influenced by gold futures over time.

DCC-GARCH Modeling and Dynamic Correlation

After conducting the ARCH effect tests, we established a DCC-GARCH model for the gold futures and spot returns. We first determined the model orders. Based on the autocorrelation and partial autocorrelation plots of the squared residual series, we selected an order of (1,1) for the model.

Dynamic correlation coefficient graphs revealed a consistent positive correlation between gold futures and spot markets, indicating a sustained positive relationship. The correlation coefficient fluctuated around 0.95, signifying a high degree of correlation. During the first half of 2019, the dynamic correlation coefficient experienced three noticeable drops, reaching 0.85, 0.87, and 0.8, followed by rapid returns to the mean. The second half of 2019 saw a relatively stable dynamic correlation coefficient, and around January 2020, a significant drop in the correlation coefficient occurred, followed by a sharp rebound. During this time, external factors negatively affected trading in the gold futures and spot markets, leading to a drastic decrease in the dynamic correlation. Nevertheless, the dynamic correlation remained above 0.5, indicating that there was still a substantial degree of interaction between the two markets. In summary, the gold futures and spot markets exhibited a highly positive and sustained correlation, even during turbulent periods.
