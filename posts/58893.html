The Impact of Index Investing on the Price of Agricultural Commodities Based on MIDAS
#Competition 2018-11-12

Highlight:
Team Leader and Independent Learning in the Sophomore Year.

In my sophomore year, I took the lead in a project, despite just having begun my journey with R software and independently studying the MIDAS Frequency Mixing Model using domestic and international research materials. Although time constraints prevented publication, I later enhanced the content and participated in a provincial-level financial innovation competition.

The research approached the subject from the perspective of the financialization of commodity markets, specifically focusing on the impact of index investments on price volatility in the agricultural commodity market.

We gathered daily and monthly price indices data from June 2006 to August 2018 for commodities like corn, wheat, Indica rice, soybeans, cotton (cottonseeds), and rapeseed, along with the corresponding SSE Composite Index data for analysis.

We conducted descriptive statistics on the data and used interpolation to handle missing values. Using R’s plot package, we visually represented the yield trends for various commodities and indices.

To deepen our study, we built a two-factor volatility component model known as the DCC-MIDAS model, delving into the interplay between index-based investments and the price volatility of agricultural commodities. We further employed the GARCH-MIDAS model, based on realized volatility, to decompose volatility into long-term and short-term components. We conducted separate regressions, taking into account structural characteristics of price spread volatility, thereby revealing the influence of index-based investors on agricultural commodity prices.

Finally, the research presented several recommendations for mitigating agricultural commodity price volatility, including:

  1. The direct impact of index-based investors’ investment decisions on commodity price volatility.
  2. Different agricultural commodities have different reflection degrees on index-based investment impact.
  3. The strengthening linkage between index-based investments and agricultural commodities.
